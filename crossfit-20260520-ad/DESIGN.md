# CrossFit 2026-05-20 Video Design

## Style Prompt

A 4K vertical CrossFit training film with the May 19 WHOOP-tech cinematic language: sharp title punches, translucent performance panels, WHOOP green data, red/orange impact hits, scan-line texture, and a brighter cinematic grade. The edit should open on the C2 bike, build through wall balls that clearly cross the upper red target line, close pull-up effort, a clean real-time wall-ball sit-down beat, and a final push. WHOOP data appears as premium glass HUD graphics at selected moments, not as a full-time dashboard.

## Colors

- `#090c0a` translucent black for glass WHOOP panels and edge shade
- `#f4f1e8` warm white text
- `#c7ff00` WHOOP green for data, progress, and pulse accents
- `#ff3b1f` impact red for hit and transition accents
- `#16d7ff` cool cyan for secondary biometric lines

## Typography

- Display: `Impact`, `Arial Black`, system sans-serif
- Data: `Menlo`, `Consolas`, monospace

## Motion Rules

- Build from slow preparation into faster cuts.
- Use crop zooms, push-ins, shake, short flashes, and speed-line accents around action beats.
- Use visible slow motion for pull-up effort and selected wall-ball release moments, with more frequent punchy transitions.
- Keep the wall-ball sit-down at real speed so it reads as a quick human beat, not a dragged-out gag.
- Pull-up footage must be cropped tight so the athlete is the subject, not a small figure in the gym.
- All wall-ball throws except the sit-down gag should include the ball fully crossing the upper red target line and hold enough of the endpoint to make the completion clear.
- Avoid source ranges where bystanders block the athlete or the ball path.
- Keep the film look premium and bright enough to see muscle, sweat, ball path, and gym texture; do not repeat the very dark May 19 grade.
- WHOOP HUDs appear briefly at useful moments only, with the May 19 look: translucent cards, diagonal scan lines, zone bars, green status text, and thin bottom telemetry.
- Use video and audio fade-in/fade-out to make the piece feel finished.

## What Not To Do

- Do not leave data on screen continuously.
- Do not cover the athlete during key wall-ball or pull-up motion.
- Do not crush the shadows into a dark-black look.
- Do not use bright generic fitness colors or decorative gradients.
- Do not make the fall moment feel mean or slapstick-heavy.
