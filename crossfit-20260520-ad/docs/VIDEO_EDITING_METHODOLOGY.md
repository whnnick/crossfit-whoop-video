# CrossFit Short Editing Methodology

Use this as the repeatable process for future CrossFit videos.

## 1. Lock The Brief

Define these before cutting:

- Platform: vertical short video.
- Output: 9:16, 2160 x 3840, 30fps.
- Duration: usually 45-60 seconds.
- Tone: cinematic, high-energy, athletic, not a casual vlog.
- Non-negotiable shots: list exact source files and approximate timecodes.

If a requested shot is unclear, pause and inspect the source before editing.

## 2. Build A Story Arc

Use a simple training arc:

1. Atmosphere: 3-5 seconds.
2. Engine: bike, row, ski, or warm-up intensity.
3. Power: wall ball, barbell, pull-up, sprint, or explosive movement.
4. Fatigue: breathing, reset, chalk, stare, hands, sweat.
5. Contrast: funny or human moment if available.
6. Finish: final push, completed rep, or exhausted recovery.
7. Data summary: WHOOP closing card.

Avoid filling the cut with rest moments unless they are intentionally used for fatigue or humor.

## 3. Shot Selection Rules

Prioritize complete actions:

- At least 80% of action shots should show a clear start, effort, and finish.
- For wall balls, prefer reps where the ball visibly clears the target line.
- For pull-ups, zoom/crop so the athlete is the subject, not the room.
- For bike shots, use close, kinetic frames where face, arms, and fan movement read immediately.
- Keep funny moments short unless the whole concept is comedic.

## 4. Motion Design

Use edits to create camera movement:

- Zoom in during effort.
- Pan upward during wall ball throws.
- Push closer during fatigue.
- Use quick flash cuts at scene changes.
- Add brief speed changes only when they support impact.

Do not over-stack effects. One clear movement plus one transition is usually enough.

## 5. WHOOP Placement

WHOOP should appear selectively, not all the time:

- Opening: 3-6 seconds, broad training context.
- Mid-section: 1-4 short heart-rate/data hits near high intensity.
- Ending: 4-8 seconds, summary card.

Avoid large WHOOP overlays during funny moments unless the joke is data-related.

## 6. Audio

Use music as the spine, but keep gym sound alive:

- Music drives cuts and transitions.
- Ambient sound adds realism.
- SFX marks transitions, impacts, and major beats.
- Fade in/out at the start and end.

If the music is from a screen recording, extract it once and reuse the same music bed.

## 7. Quality Check

Before delivering, always verify:

- `ffprobe` reports 2160 x 3840, 30fps, correct duration.
- Opening hook is strong.
- Required clips are present.
- WHOOP appears only in planned windows.
- Text does not cover the main action.
- Audio fades cleanly.
- Final file plays from the exported location.
