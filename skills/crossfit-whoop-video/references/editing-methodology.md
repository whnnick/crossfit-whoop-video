# Editing Methodology

## 1. Lock The Brief

Confirm platform, duration, output specs, required clips, music source, source audio handling, and WHOOP data requirements. If a required moment is unclear, inspect source footage before cutting.

## 2. Build A Story Arc

Use this repeatable arc:

1. Atmosphere or hook.
2. Engine work.
3. Explosive movement.
4. Fatigue/reset.
5. Human or funny contrast when available.
6. Final push.
7. WHOOP data summary.

## 3. Select Shots

Prioritize complete actions. Avoid filling the video with moments after the rep is already finished unless they intentionally show fatigue or humor.

For common CrossFit movements:

- Wall ball: include full squat, throw, ball travel, and target-line clearance.
- Pull-up: crop tighter and keep the athlete central.
- Bike: prefer close shots with visible face, arms, fan, and cadence.
- Barbell: use start-to-lockout or setup-to-finish movement.

## 4. Create Motion

Use zoom, pan, speed ramps, flash cuts, and impact zooms to create professional camera movement. Keep effects tied to action beats.

## 5. Mix Audio

Use music as the spine. Retain ambient gym sound for realism. Add SFX for transition hits, but keep them subordinate to the main rhythm.

## 6. Place WHOOP

WHOOP overlays should appear in short windows:

- opening context
- mid-intensity telemetry
- ending summary

Avoid large HUD panels over the athlete during important action.

## 7. Verify

Run `ffprobe`, sample frames at required moments, and check the final output manually. Verify duration, resolution, fps, audio, required clips, and WHOOP visibility.
